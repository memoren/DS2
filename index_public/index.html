An investigation of video game sales and playtime between 2004 and 2010

By Debbie Hsu, Vishva Patel, Jiahao Zhao, Steve Wang, Matthew Moreno, Zheyuan Zhang

Analysis

Company's Specialty

Action games is the mainstream of the market, and the king of action game sales is Nintendo, which also has a leading position in racing, RPG, simulation, and strategy games. Besides Nintendo, other publishers are also doing well in multiple genres. EA and 2K are far ahead in sports games; EA and Microsoft both perform well in various types of games.

Time Killer

In line with most people’s experience, role-playing games take the most time, followed by action and driving games. Microsoft’s games especially are especially time-consuming compared to other publishers. In the racing and sports genre, Atari and Konami take the most time, respectively. Surprisingly, other games in the dataset takes up about the same time.

Persuasion

Killer Game

An electronic game that has compelled many to buy a particular video game console or upgrade their computer hardware simply to play it.

Company Overview

For console manufacturers like Nintendo and SONY, making their own killer-game and featuring it exclusively on their console is an effective way of increasing console sales.

In this project, we investigate how effective this strategy is by comparing three major console manufacturers: Nintendo (DS & Wii), SONY (PSP & PS3), and Microsoft (Xbox360).

Between 2004 and 2010, Nintendo and Microsoft had much greater sales compared to SONY even if they have about the same number of games published on each platform.

Top Games

A closer look at the sales of each game on each platform shows that both Nintendo & Microsoft’s best-selling game is made by themselves and published on their platform exclusively.

Top Publishers

This is also reflected in the sales of each publisher. Nintendo & Microsoft is the best-selling publisher on their own platform.
